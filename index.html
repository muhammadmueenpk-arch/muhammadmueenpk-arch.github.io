About Me

From Physical Infrastructure
to Enterprise Networks

I began my career on the front lines of infrastructure — installing fiber optic networks, structured cabling systems, and low-voltage building systems across Pakistan and Saudi Arabia. Over 6 years, I have built a deep understanding of how data flows from the physical layer up: from fiber splicing and OTDR testing to rack installations, patch panel termination, and data center cabling.

Today, I work with Building Management Systems (BMS), Fire Alarm, Access Control, and CCTV at enterprise-grade facilities including Magrabi Hospital and Four Seasons Jeddah. My daily work involves coordinating with MEP teams, managing cable infrastructure across multi-floor deployments, and ensuring every termination meets certification standards.

What sets me apart: While many network engineers understand routing and switching in theory, I have hands-on experience with the physical infrastructure that carries their packets. I know how fiber behaves in ducting, how to troubleshoot splice loss with an OTDR, and how to design cable pathways that scale. This Layer 0-1 expertise gives me a unique foundation as I transition into enterprise networking.

I am currently studying CCNA to formalize my networking knowledge and plan to pursue CCNP Enterprise followed by Network Security specialization. My long-term goal is to become a Network Security Engineer who can design, implement, and secure end-to-end enterprise infrastructure.
